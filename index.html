About him

Dedicated and ambitious for passionate professional and personal growth, Pau Sabater Nácher is an undergraduate student of two simultaneous Bachelor Degrees in the fields of Mathematics and Physics Engineering at Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya under CFIS mentorizing. He was born in Algemesí, a small town in Valencia, Spain, until he received a residence grant for continuing his studies in Barcelona. Driven by a desire to make a meaningful impact, he has recently invested his time learning Machine Learning and Hardware Design, after being intern at an industry-leading start-up in California and a very recognized Institute of Technology in the metropolitan area of Barcelona. However, thanks to an active childhood, he needs to seamlessly weave calisthenics into his routine, rejoins his hometown's handball squad upon every homecoming, and rides the surging waves of the Mediterranean whenever its spirit awakens. He also relaxes by dancing fingers across both the violin's strings and the piano's keys, and he yearly travels with friends and family around the world to find out new cultures, experiences and perspectives.

Hardware Engineer | High-Dynamics Robotics, Mobile Robotics and Intelligent Systems department, Institut de Robòtica i Informàtica Industrial

Researcher in the field of perception and control for agile robotics, implementing a thrust control algorithm in a custom designed board (PCB). The purpose of this project is the development of robots with highly dynamic movement capabilities, using optimal control and model-based predictive control (MPC). The role included electronic PCB design with Kicad and microcontroller programming with STM32. For perception, state estimation and sensory fusion tools developed by our group were used. The project has several collaborators, including undergraduate, master's and doctoral students, and post-docs. The group has also established links with reference laboratories in the manufacture and control of complex robots, such as the LAAS-CNRS in France or the PAL robotics company in Barcelona.

Formula Student team electronic engineer membership. Development of a formula-style racing car for non-professional drivers who would participate in single-make races. Each student team designs and builds the prototype within the competition regulations, which has two main objectives: to encourage creative solutions and to guarantee safety on the track. PCB design (with Proteus) and microcontroller programming (with C) for legal car improvement regulations under competition requirements updates. DC-AC inverter design planification so as to substitute sponsor's one. Technical and economic reports development. Team logistic management for international competitions.
